// 01 — Career Exploration

Understanding the Field

🛡️ Cybersecurity Analyst

A Cybersecurity Analyst protects an organisation's digital infrastructure by monitoring networks for threats, investigating security incidents, and implementing defensive controls. They sit at the intersection of technical expertise and strategic thinking — analysing attack patterns, conducting vulnerability assessments, and responding to breaches in real time.

In today's landscape, cyber threats grow at an exponential rate — from ransomware targeting critical infrastructure to state-sponsored espionage. Malaysia's national cybersecurity framework and rapid digital transformation across banking, aviation, and healthcare sectors have made this one of the most in-demand roles in the country.

My academic training at UniSZA in network security, forensics, and penetration testing — combined with real CTF competition experience and a Final Year Project in blockchain-based secure evidence sharing — gives me a strong and differentiated foundation for this career.

Key Responsibilities

  • Monitor SIEM tools for anomalies & intrusions
  • Conduct vulnerability assessments & pen tests
  • Investigate & respond to security incidents
  • Analyse malware and threat intelligence
  • Maintain security policies & compliance
  • Perform digital forensics investigations
  • Produce security reports for stakeholders

Technical Skills Required by Industry

  • SIEM tools — Splunk, IBM QRadar, Sentinel
  • Network packet analysis — Wireshark, tcpdump
  • Scripting — Python, Bash automation
  • Pen testing frameworks — Metasploit, Burp Suite
  • Firewall & IDS/IPS configuration
  • Cloud security — AWS, Azure fundamentals
  • Digital forensics — Autopsy, FTK Imager
